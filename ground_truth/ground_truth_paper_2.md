**#4** ***Pectis stella***

comment accepted May 2026

[![file](https://pubpeer.com/storage/image-1778606134750.png)](https://pubpeer.com/storage/image-1778606134750.png)

**Concern regarding source data for Figure 8f (MOESM11)** In the supplementary source data file MOESM11 (Fig. 8f), which reports CD14⁺FCN1⁺ cell ratios from 40 paired PT/RT patient samples, we identified a pattern that cannot arise from genuine independent measurements. Finding: For all 20 comparisons between pair N and pair N+20, the RT/PT ratio is identical to 4 decimal places:

• Pair 1: RT/PT = 20.7445 — Pair 21: RT/PT = 20.7445

• Pair 8: RT/PT = 3.0503 — Pair 28: RT/PT = 3.0503

• Pair 10: RT/PT = 24.7347 — Pair 30: RT/PT = 24.7347

• Pair 20: RT/PT = 6.9725 — Pair 40: RT/PT = 6.9725

This holds for all 20 pairs (20/20). The data is consistent with the following formula:

RT(N+20) = PT(N+20) × \[RT(N) / PT(N)\]

Verification: multiplying each PT value in pairs 21–40 by the corresponding ratio from pairs 1–20 reproduces the recorded RT values exactly (difference = 0.000000 for all 20 pairs).

Additionally, 12 of the 40 pairs show RT = exactly 2.000× PT.

These patterns suggest that pairs 21–40 were not independently measured but mathematically derived from pairs 1–20, meaning the reported n=40 cohort may contain only ~20 genuine patients. We encourage the editors to request original instrument output files for all 40 paired samples.

**Concern regarding source data for Figure 6i (MOESM9)**

In the supplementary source data file MOESM9 (Fig. 6i), which reports R\_CD8-ZNF683 values from 40 paired PT/RT patient samples, we identified multiple patterns inconsistent with genuine independent measurements.

Finding 1 — N vs N+20 ratio duplication (19/20 pairs):

For 19 out of 20 comparisons between pair N and pair N+20, the RT/PT ratio is identical to 4 decimal places. Selected examples:

• Pair 1: RT/PT = 1.4545 — Pair 21: RT/PT = 1.4545

• Pair 3: RT/PT = 7.3333 — Pair 23: RT/PT = 7.3333

• Pair 10: RT/PT = 24.0000 — Pair 30: RT/PT = 24.0000

• Pair 20: RT/PT = 38.0000 — Pair 40: RT/PT = 38.0000

This is consistent with the \[...\] formula:

RT(N+20) = PT(N+20) × \[RT(N) / PT(N)\]

This formula can be verified directly from the MOESM9 file: multiplying each PT value in pairs 21–40 by the corresponding ratio from pairs 1–20 reproduces the recorded RT values exactly.

Finding 2 — RT = exactly 2.000× PT (10/40 pairs):

Pairs 5, 12, 13, 14, 19, 25, 32, 33, 34, and 39 all show RT = exactly 2.000× PT (verified to 4 decimal places). Ten pairs sharing this exact ratio cannot arise by chance.

Conclusion:

The N vs N+20 pattern (19/20 matches) is structurally identical to the pattern identified in Fig. 8f of the same paper (20/20 matches, see separate comment). Both datasets are consistent with pairs 21–40 being mathematically derived from pairs 1–20, rather than representing independent patient measurements. The reported cohort size of n=40 may therefore reflect only ~20 genuine patients, invalidating the associated statistical analyses. We encourage the editors to request original instrument output files (e.g. raw .fcs files or image analysis export logs with timestamps) for all 40 paired samples to verify the authenticity of these measurements.\*\*

**#8** ***Plasmodium odocoilei***

comment accepted May 2026

**Concern: Figure 3f source data**

We examined the source data (Supplementary Data 6, Sheet "Fig.3f") for the 40 paired PT/RT samples in Validation Cohort 1.

Pairs 21–40 appear to be copies of Pairs 1–20 with selective modifications:

1.  60% of PT values (12/20) and 50% of RT values (10/20) in Pairs 21–40 are numerically identical to Pairs 1–20, matching to 10 decimal places (e.g., Pair 8 PT: 0.0071069160; Pair 12 PT: 0.0088770200; Pair 19 RT: 0.0488033660 — all exactly reproduced).
2.  Every modified value is a round number (0.02, 0.04, 0.06, 0.08, or 0.10), whereas original values from Pairs 1–20 carry 8–10 significant figures as expected from image analysis software output. Round numbers account for only 25% of Pairs 1–20 but 61% of Pairs 21–40 (χ² p = 0.004).
3.  All PT modifications are upward (8/8), and 9/10 RT modifications are upward, consistently by ~2 percentage points.

We ask the authors to provide original image analysis output files for all 40 patients to verify that Pairs 21–40 represent independent measurements.

**#9** ***Plasmodium odocoilei***

comment accepted May 2026

**Concern: Figure 7d source data**

In the source data for Figure 7d (Supplementary Data 6), which reports the ratio of CD20⁺ cells in 40 paired PT/RT samples from Validation Cohort 1, Pairs 21–40 are not independent measurements — they are Pairs 1–20 multiplied by a constant factor of exactly 1.2.

Specifically, for all 20 PT values: Pair (20+n) PT ÷ Pair n PT = 1.200000000 without exception (standard deviation of ratios = 0.0000). The same holds for all 20 RT values. A uniform scaling factor of precisely 1.2, applied identically to all 40 values across two independent variables, cannot arise from biological measurement.

Restricting the analysis to Pairs 1–20 alone remains statistically significant (paired t-test, p = 0.0018), indicating that the duplication inflated the reported sample size from 20 to 40 without altering the conclusion. We ask the authors to provide original raw measurement records confirming that Pairs 21–40 represent independent patients distinct from Pairs 1–20.

**#12** ***Miobantia fuscata***

comment accepted June 2026

[![file](https://pubpeer.com/storage/image-1780693463453.png)](https://pubpeer.com/storage/image-1780693463453.png) [![file](https://pubpeer.com/storage/image-1780693525051.png)](https://pubpeer.com/storage/image-1780693525051.png)

We wish to raise a data integrity concern regarding the proliferation assay data presented in Extended Data Fig. 3g. This figure shows cell proliferation curves for C666 and TW03 cell lines under three conditions (PBS, MCAM(−), and MCAM(+)) across five time points (24–120 h), with three biological replicates per group. Upon examining the PBS control group, we noted that all 15 paired differences between corresponding data points of the two cell lines are restricted exclusively to ±0.01 or ±0.02 OD units: 8 pairs differ by −0.02, 5 pairs by +0.02, and 2 pairs by +0.01. No paired difference exceeds 0.02 OD units. This pattern is difficult to reconcile with truly independent experimental replicates. The intra-group coefficients of variation within this same dataset range from approximately 1.5% to 26%, demonstrating that individual measurements do exhibit meaningful biological and technical variability. It is therefore statistically implausible that 15 independent paired comparisons between two distinct cell lines would all fall within a ±0.02 OD range by chance alone. We wish to emphasize that we raise this as a question rather than an assertion. There may be an explanation we are not aware of. We would welcome clarification from the authors, and suggest that sharing the original raw data files (e.g., plate reader output with acquisition timestamps) would be the most straightforward way to address this concern.
